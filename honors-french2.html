Welcome to French 2 Honors

Learning French isn’t just about vocabulary and grammar—it’s about unlocking a whole new world. In French 2 Honors, you go beyond the basics and start truly connecting with the language, the culture, and the people who speak it. Each phrase you learn, each conversation you practice, brings you closer to understanding not just a language—but a way of life.

French is spoken on five continents, in over 29 countries. It’s a global language of diplomacy, fashion, cuisine, art, and ideas. When you speak it, you become part of a much larger conversation. In this class, you’ll explore real-life situations—traveling, dining, navigating school or social life—and gain the confidence to use French in meaningful ways. Whether you're talking about weekend plans, telling a story, or describing your environment, your voice in French starts to take shape.

But French 2 is more than just practice. It’s discovery. You’ll explore the rich history, music, traditions, and literature of the Francophone world—from Paris to Senegal, Quebec to Morocco. You’ll begin to see the world through different eyes and recognize the shared humanity that connects us all, even across oceans and borders.

And yes, there will be challenges—tricky verb tenses, unfamiliar accents, or moments when you blank on a word. But every mistake is part of the learning process. In fact, it’s how you learn. The key is to stay curious and courageous. With each lesson, your understanding grows, and suddenly things that once felt hard start to click.

Language learning builds focus, discipline, and empathy. It trains your brain to think in new ways and strengthens your memory and communication skills. It also gives you an edge—in college, in careers, and in life. People who speak a second language stand out. They travel more easily, connect more deeply, and see the world with greater nuance.

So lean in. Listen closely. Speak boldly. Let yourself be surprised by how much you can understand—and how much more you can say. French 2 Honors is your gateway to a global identity, where your voice matters not just in English, but in one of the most beautiful, expressive languages on Earth.

Why Continue Studying French?

Learning a language is like unlocking a second way of seeing the world. In French 2 Honors, you’ll go beyond simple phrases and greetings — you’ll start to express yourself with detail, confidence, and personality.

This course helps you expand your communication skills, understand French-speaking cultures more deeply, and build a strong foundation for advanced language study — or even international travel!

What Is French 2 Honors All About?

French 2 builds on what you learned in French 1 and takes things further: more grammar, more vocabulary, more fluency. The Honors level adds extra challenge, with higher expectations for listening, speaking, reading, and writing.

You’ll work toward being able to:

  • Describe daily routines, likes/dislikes, future plans, and past experiences
  • Understand spoken and written French at a more natural pace
  • Hold short conversations with more fluidity and detail
  • Write short paragraphs using correct grammar and varied vocabulary
  • Explore French-speaking countries and cultural practices

Why Take French 2 Honors?

  • It Sharpens Your Mind: Language learning boosts memory, focus, and cognitive flexibility.
  • It Builds Global Awareness: Learn how people live, think, and celebrate in Francophone cultures.
  • It Prepares You for AP French: Honors sets you up for the rigor and fluency needed in advanced levels.
  • It’s Personally Empowering: Express yourself in another language — and open doors to travel, careers, and new friendships.

What to Expect

In French 2 Honors, you’ll engage with:

  • Grammar: Past tense (passé composé), future tense, reflexive verbs, and object pronouns
  • Vocabulary: Travel, food, school life, technology, daily routine, health, holidays
  • Speaking practice: Conversations, role plays, and oral presentations
  • Listening: Audio recordings, dialogues, and short videos
  • Culture: Learn about French-speaking countries across Europe, Africa, the Caribbean, and Canada

How to Succeed in French 2 Honors

  • Review French 1 content: Don’t lose the basics — review key verbs, sentence structures, and greetings.
  • Practice regularly: Daily short sessions are more effective than cramming.
  • Use it actively: Try speaking aloud at home or journaling in French.
  • Listen to real French: Songs, videos, and native speakers help build your ear for the language.
  • Ask for help: Don’t be afraid to ask questions — every mistake is a step toward fluency.

Summer To-Do List: Get Ready for French 2 Honors

  • Review your French 1 notebook or textbook: Focus on present tense verb conjugations (avoir, être, aller, faire), regular verbs, and common adjectives.
  • Practice vocabulary with Quizlet: Look up French 1 review sets for travel, food, greetings, and basic questions.
  • Listen to French music or watch French videos: Try “Learn French with Alexa,” “Comme une Française,” or beginner Netflix shows with subtitles (e.g., Miraculous Ladybug).
  • Try Duolingo or a language app: Use 5–10 minutes a day to stay in practice.
  • Label objects in your house in French: Tape index cards to items like “la porte,” “le miroir,” “la fenêtre.”
  • Optional: Write a few journal entries in French about your summer — even simple sentences are great practice!
